Banff, Canada

Brief Description


The Town of Banff was the first town in Canada to be incorporated inside a national park when it did so in 1990. Banff and Lake Louise, which are home to renowned mountain ranges, provide countless possibilities to experience the breathtaking beauty all year long. Discovering the special magic of the Canadian Rockies is made possible by the region's sparkling emerald lakes, breathtaking snow-capped peaks, and innumerable picturesque vantage points.

People & Culture


A thriving center for the arts and culture, Banff has attracted artists for years thanks to its inspiring surroundings. In addition to numerous private art galleries and live music venues, Banff is home to cultural treasures like the Whyte Museum of the Canadian Rockies and the Banff Centre.



Best Places to Visit


The places I want to visit in Banff, Canada are their breathtaking and soothing lakes. With turquoise colored water, surrounded by steep glacial mountains.

Iceland

Brief Description


Iceland is a land of numerous wonders, known as the "land of ice and fire." Gorgeous natural scenery, glaciers, volcanoes, and geysers can be found there. Iceland is a well-liked tourist destination, which should come as no surprise given its stunning villages and towns.

People & Culture


Due the country's small area, family is very important in Iceland. Icelandic families are frequently bigger than those in other nations in Northern Europe. Although members of the extended family don't typically reside together, grandparents, uncles, and aunts frequently assist with child care. Since most individuals in Iceland have lived there for a very long time, many Icelanders enjoy researching their ancestry.


In Icelandic culture and custom, everyone in the family participates in household chores. Icelandic adolescents are raised to be independent, and as such, they are required to clean their own bedrooms, change their own linens, vacuum or wash their floors, do their own washing, cook their own meals, and clean their kitchens after every usage.

Best Places to Visit


The magnificent landscapes of this extraordinary country include snow-capped mountains, active volcanoes, pristine lakes, and river valleys, which are the things I love the most in the nature.


Glacier Lagoon


One of Iceland's most stunning locations is the Jokulsarlon Glasier Lagoon. The lagoon is surrounded by breathtaking glaciers and rugged mountains. They over hiking opportunities where visitors may take in the breathtaking environment and catch a glimpse of the Northern Lights!

Norway

Brief Description


Norway is a small nation located in northern Europe. The famed fjords, which are marine inlets tucked between high cliffs, are found along Norway's coastline. These fjords and mountains are sculpted out by glaciers that are breathtaking to look at.

People & Culture


Norwegians take great pride in their democratic and humane values. The nation frequently accepts refugees and asylum seekers from other nations. Norway's citizens enjoy one of the greatest standards of living in the entire globe. Typically, businesses open at 8 a.m. Till 2 p.m., with a 15-minute lunch break. Both parents are eligible for 42 weeks of paid leave when a child is born. The official start date for school in Norway is seven years old (National Geographic, n.d.).

Best Places to Visit


Great views of fjords, mountains, and northern lights are the reasons I want to visit this country. Norway possesses many mountains, and some of them are too steep that no one has ever tried to climb them.

Another amazing thing about Norway is its extraordinary view of the Aurora Borealis. Between late September and late March, Norway becomes dark from early afternoon until late morning when the northern lights soar across the atmosphere as if a cosmic ballet of light were being performed throughout the night sky.

Ireland

Brief Description


Ireland sits off of the northern coast of continental Europe and West of Great Britain. It is referred to as the Emerald Isle due to its wide swaths of beautiful, green fields. There also exist huge stretches of rocky, uneven terrain, and amazing rock formations across this country.

People & Culture


Ireland is a country of storytellers. The custom originated with Celtic bards who used to write down and recite the nation's history. Irish culture is fascinating, varied, and a joy to learn about. Myths, literature, music, dancing, art, gastronomy, and language are all part of it. The culture was once only Gaelic, but it later absorbed elements from the English, Tudor English, Scots, Normans, and Vikings.

Best Places to Visit


One of my dream destinations here is the Cliffs of Moher. For years, its natural beauty has captivated scientists, geologists, painters, musicians, and poets who have been lured by the cliff’s distinctive landscape. In this terrain, you can see the mesmerizing view of the Aran Islands, as well as the calming view, and at the same time chaotic waves of the Atlantic Ocean.

Rock of Cashel


The Rock, rising high above the Golden Vale, is home to an intriguing collection of ancient religious artifacts. According to mythology, the Rock of Cashel originally gained political importance in the fourth or fifth century AD when Eógan Mór's heirs built a substantial stronghold and formed a line of kings-cum-bishops who ruled over this region of Munster.

Killarney National Park


Killarney is another remarkable place in Ireland. The lakeshores are covered in untouched woodland, including oak, yew, and Mediterranean flora like the arbutus, also known as the strawberry tree because of its bright red but poisonous berries. The sole wild herd of red deer in Ireland, as well as otter, pine marten, red squirrels, and Irish hare, are significant creatures found in the park. One of the best things to do in Ireland is to visit Killarney.

Lapland, Finland

Brief Description


Lapland, Finland's most northern district, is a popular outdoor vacation spot famous for Santa Claus, amazing year-round light shows, and the vastness of the arctic environment. The northern portion of the region is also covered by the native Sámi people's homeland.

People & Culture


Finns are typically polite and open-minded. Families are often tiny, consisting of just one or two kids. Punctuality, politeness, and practicality are valued in Finnish society.
